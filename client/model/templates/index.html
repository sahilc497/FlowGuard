Crop Water & Nutrient Best Practices

💧 Water Saving Measures

  • Adopt drip irrigation to minimise evaporation and conveyance losses.
  • Schedule irrigation for early morning or late evening to reduce evaporative demand.
  • Perform soil moisture checks and avoid unnecessary irrigations during early vegetative stages.
  • Use mulch where possible to retain soil moisture and suppress weeds.
  • Group fields by crop and growth stage to optimise irrigation scheduling.

🌱 Fertilizer Management

  • Split nitrogen applications for high-demand crops (sugarcane, maize) to reduce leaching.
  • Prefer soil tests to determine exact nutrient needs before blanket application.
  • Use organic amendments (compost, FYM) to improve soil structure and water-holding capacity.
  • Avoid applying high doses of fertilizer immediately before heavy irrigation or rainfall.
  • Follow recommended doses for each crop; over-application wastes resources and harms soil health.

🌾 Crop Health & Field Practices

  • Ensure proper field leveling for uniform water distribution and reduced pooling.
  • Rotate crops to manage pests and improve soil fertility over seasons.
  • Use disease-resistant varieties and timely pest scouting to reduce yield loss.
  • Monitor leaf colour and growth regularly — sudden changes often indicate water or nutrient stress.
  • Record irrigation events and water volumes to build a farm-specific dataset for better decisions.

📋 Practical On-Farm Tips

  • Keep a simple logbook (date, area irrigated, duration, water source) — this improves scheduling over time.
  • Use local weather forecasts to skip irrigations before predicted heavy rainfall.
  • Calibrate pumps and measure actual discharge to estimate accurate water usage per hour.
  • Train farm workers on best irrigation timings and fertilizer placement for uniform application.
  • Consider on-farm demonstrations for new farmers to showcase water-saving technologies.

Operator guidance: combine these best practices with FlowGuard predictions to reduce water usage while maintaining yields.
